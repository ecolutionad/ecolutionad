CARBON FOOTPRINT

A carbon footprint is the total amount of greenhouse gas emissions that come from the production, use and end-of-life of a product or service which trap heat in the atmosphere, causing global warming.

More information

Evidence

Global Temperature Rise

The planet's average surface temperature has risen about 1.62 degrees Fahrenheit (0.9 degrees Celsius) since the late 19th century, a change driven largely by increased carbon dioxide and other human-made emissions into the atmosphere.

Warning Oceans

The oceans have absorbed much of this increased heat, with the top 700 meters (about 2,300 feet) of ocean showing warming of more than 0.4 degrees Fahrenheit since 1969.

Shrinking Ice Sheets

The Greenland and Antarctic ice sheets have decreased in mass. Data from NASA's Gravity Recovery and Climate Experiment show Greenland lost an average of 286 billion tons of ice per year between 1993 and 2016.

Causes

The amount of carbon emissions trapped in our atmosphere causes global warming, which causes climate change, symptoms of which include melting of the polar ice caps, the rising of sea levels, the disturbance of animals' natural habitats, extreme weather events,...

  • + Polar Ice Caps

    Polar ice caps may not seem important to our daily lives but there are many animals who live there. Without their habitat, their species could possibly become extinct. Global warming causes the polar ice caps to melt which is not good for those animals.

  • + Sea Levels

    The rising of sea levels is a big problem in climate change. There are many effects which include flooding, loss of marshlands and erosion of beaches. Some things that cause rising of sea levels include melting ice, thermal expansion and land sinkage.

  • + Animals' Natural Habitats

    Some of the animals living in their natural habitats are becoming extinct. This is because we are doing harmful things to their home without possibly knowing. For example the polar bears live in the polar ice caps and those are melting which causes them to lose their habitat.

  • + Weather events

    We do not change or control the weather but some forms of weather can destroy habitats including our habitats as humans. Trees will fall, nests will be destroyed and much more.
